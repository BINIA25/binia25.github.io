About the Conference

Background and Rationale

Ethiopia's contemporary political discourse is shaped by deep historical legacies, contested narratives of nationhood, and diverging visions for the future. From the imperial era to the revolutionary period and the federal experiment, the country has experienced repeated cycles of political settlement and fragmentation.

The National Dialogue process now underway offers a rare opportunity to engage constructively with these unresolved questions. However, dialogue alone is not sufficient. Without an agreed framework to guide the eventual formation of a Grand Narrative, the outcomes risk remaining fragmented.

Conference Objectives

Establish Consensus

Build broad consensus on the need for a unifying narrative that reflects Ethiopia's diversity while guiding its shared future.

Map Frameworks

Identify institutional, policy, and structural arrangements necessary to support the Grand Narrative formation.

Define Pathway

Outline a coordinated process for moving from dialogue to institutionalized consensus.

Expected Outcomes

Jima Consensus Statement

Affirming the national need for a Grand Narrative and outlining guiding principles.

Framework Roadmap

Identifying priority reforms and institutional measures across policy and governance.

Partnership Map

Detailing roles of government, academia, and media in advancing the work.
